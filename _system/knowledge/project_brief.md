MY ITALIANVILLA
The Italian villa, as an architectural form, is a cultural artefact that encapsulates centuries of interaction
between human habitation, landscape, and climate.
From antiquity through the Renaissance, the villa has provided a spatial and material language  that ba-
lances permanence with adaptability and monumentality with intimacy. Throughout history, villas such
as Villa Adriana in Tivoli, Villa Medici in Rome, and Villa La Rotonda near Vicenza have shaped an Italian
villa typology characterised by clarity of form, environmental responsiveness, and the integration of
architecture with the landscape.
Today, the villa represents a compelling model, not as a nostalgic reconstruction of the past but as a living
typology that harmonises design and technology . Its enduring formal language, marked by symmetry,
proportion, and spatial hierarchy, is speciﬁcally compatible with contemporary sustainable design prin -
ciples. Thermal massing, natural ventilation, compact spatial planning, and elevated foundations are not
only traditional features but also strategies recognised today for their energy eﬃciency. When combined
with modern materials, passive energy systems, and discreet technological integration, the villa becomes
a space where historical continuity meets environmental innovation.
In regions like Los Angeles, where environmental instability, especially ﬁre, is a pressing concern, the villa
oﬀers a construction model grounded in durability and resilience. Built from sustainable concrete, it can
withstand ﬂames and heat, oﬀering not only aesthetic permanence but also structural security. In con-
trast to the fragility of contemporary lightweight construction, the villa endures—anchored in the landsca-
pe, shaped by it, and protective of its inhabitants.
Looking toward the villa today is not to retreat into the past; rather, it is an opportunity to recover a set of
principles- beauty, balance, and environmental intelligence , that remain profoundly relevant to the fu-
ture of architecture.

IT’S is a research and innovation-
oriented architecture ﬁrm founded in
2016 by Alessandro Cambi, Francesco
Marinelli and Paolo Mezzalama with
oﬃces in Rome and Paris.
IT’S works on projects at various scales from urban planning
to design. The principal completed and ongoing projects
include the redevelopment of Piazza dei Cinquecento and
the urban space of Termini station in Rome; the renovation
of the former school of architecture in Nanterre on the Pole
Leonard de Vinci university campus in Paris; the renovation
of the former INPS headquarters in Via dell’ Amba Aradam in
Rome; the new headquarters of the MSA — Mutualité Sociale
Agricole — in Bordeaux; the experimental housing building in
EUR, Rome; the new FOROF centre for contemporary art by
Giovanna Caruso Fendi in Rome.
Alessandro Cambi, Francesco Marinelli and Paolo
Mezzalama have been professional project and research
partners since 2004. They were the founding members of
the Scape group, with which they worked until 2016, and
were ranked among the top 10 up-and-coming ﬁrms by the
French Ministry of Culture in 2008 — NAJA, Nouveau Album
des Jeunes Architectes — and winners in 2014 of the Young
Architecture Talent award launched by the National Council
of Italian Architects  — CNAPPC. IT’S most important awards
include the BIM & DIGITAL AWARD in 2017, the awarding
of IMGP 2 — Inventons la Metropole du Grand Paris 2 —
in 2019 and C40 — Reinventing Cities in Rome in 2023.

DottorGroup SpA is an Italian construction
company renowned for its excellence in
restoration, redevelopment, and the creation
of complex and prestigious architectural
works.
For over forty years, our mission has been to combine tradition
and innovation to oﬀer sustainable, durable, and visually impactful
building solutions. Over the years, we have built a strong identity
based on technical expertise, attention to detail, and the use of the
most advanced technologies. We primarily collaborate with private
clients, tackling challenging projects ranging from the restoration of
protected heritage to the construction of contemporary architecture.
At DGU, architectural concrete is more than just a material; it
is an expressive medium, a design language, and an identity-
deﬁning element. Every surface, every geometry, every detail is an
opportunity to shape authentic, essential, and powerful architecture.
From the early stages, we work closely with architects, engineers,
and clients to transform creative vision into a technically achievable
product.
Our philosophy is not to “mask” the material’s impact but to use it with
awareness, precision, and respect for the context. We believe that
true sustainability comes from integrated design, intelligent logistics,
and execution quality, which avoids waste, errors, and rework. Our
commitment is continuous: we constantly improve our plants, invest
in research, and work with designers and environmental consultants
to make our concrete increasingly eﬃcient, responsible, and aligned
with a contemporary architectural vision attentive to the planet.
