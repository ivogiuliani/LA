Sorry you are going through this, the post-fire environmental reports are honestly the part nobody warns you about.

On the hazmat/relo side, the names that come up most in UHNW circles are BELFOR, ATI Restoration, and Servpro's commercial division for the actual remediation, with private relocation handled through a family office travel desk or a firm like Pickfords for international moves. For the environmental piece specifically, hiring an independent industrial hygienist (CIH-certified) before you sign anything with a remediation contractor is the move, otherwise you are negotiating against their own assessment.

One longer-term thought, if you ever do rebuild on a WUI lot: the typologies that hold their insurability now are reinforced concrete shells with sealed envelopes, not the wood-frame replacements most contractors default to. Worth knowing before any rebuild conversation starts.
