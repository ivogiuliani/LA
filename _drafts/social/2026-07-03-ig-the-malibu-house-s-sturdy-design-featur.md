A widely shared account of a Malibu home credits its survival to sturdy design: stucco over cement, a non-combustible roof, and deep pilings anchored into bedrock against quake and wave. Not a miracle — a set of decisions made at the drawing board.

This is the thinking behind reinforced concrete. A non-combustible shell isn't a feature you add. It's the foundation of a home built to live with Los Angeles, not against it — quietly, for generations.
