--- SLIDE 01: COURTYARD ---
An inward-facing room without a roof — privacy, microclimate, contained ritual.

--- SLIDE 02: GIO PONTI ---
Villa Planchart, Caracas, 1957. The courtyard as orchestrated threshold — where the house turns its back on the street and opens only to itself.

--- SLIDE 03: MARCO ZANUSO ---
Casa Arzale, Arzachena, Sardegna, 1964. Stone-bound and wind-sheltered — the courtyard as climatic logic, not decorative gesture.

--- SLIDE 04: TONI FOLLINA ---
Casa Follina, Nervesa della Battaglia, Treviso, 1972. The courtyard as lived center — domestic ritual folded into the geometry of enclosure.

--- SLIDE 05: ALBERTO PONIS ---
Casa Gostner, Costa Paradiso, Sardegna, 1998. Carved from the hillside, the courtyard holds air, shadow, and silence against the Mediterranean glare.

--- SLIDE 06: MY VILLA · LOS ANGELES ---
In reinforced concrete, the courtyard becomes permanent enclosure — fire-resilient, thermally precise, and calibrated to the California microclimate. Italian Soul. Californian Body.
