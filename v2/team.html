01 The Practice
Architecture & Design · Rome · Paris · Los Angeles

IT'S Architecture

IT'S is a research- and innovation-oriented practice established in 2016 by Alessandro Cambi, Francesco Marinelli and Paolo Mezzalama. The studio works across cultural, residential and public commissions with a consistent thesis: architecture as a long-duration object — designed to outlast trends, weather, and market cycles.

My Villa is the studio's California practice and response to the climate challenges affecting the city of Los Angeles — a single brief: luxury villas that stay insurable, standing, and livable through the fire cycles ahead. The Italian villa typology — courtyard, podium, portico, pergola — reinterpreted for Malibu, Beverly Hills and the Westside, built to the 2026 California WUI Code and the IBHS Wildfire Prepared Home Plus standard from the first sketch.

The same people who design the homes curate the Journal. That is deliberate: everything we publish is reviewed by a practicing architect before it goes live.

02 How We Think

A research practice
that builds.

i. Long-duration architecture

Buildings that outlast trends, weather and market cycles. In California that translates directly into homes that stay insurable and stay standing.

ii. Typology, not nostalgia

Courtyard, podium, portico, pergola, inward-facing garden. The archetypes that made Italian villas last for centuries, re-engineered for non-combustibility and California seismic.

iii. Research before drawing

Each project begins with typological and technological research. For My Villa, that now includes California insurance economics, WUI-Code performance data, and IBHS wildfire certification.

iv. One desk, two outputs

The villas and the Journal come from the same table. We write only about what we are qualified to build — and we build what we write about.

04 The Journal Desk

Editorial standards,
not marketing volume.

The My Villa Journal is a working record of the insurance, construction, and regulatory conditions shaping luxury homebuilding in California — published at the pace of active monitoring.

Primary sources only

Every factual claim traces to a named publication, government agency, or public filing — with a deep link, not a homepage.

No speculation without attribution

If we don't have a source, we don't make the claim.

Conflict disclosure

We design and build reinforced-concrete, non-combustible villas; when we write about that category, we say so. Our perspective is informed, not neutral.

Practitioner review & corrections

No article publishes without sign-off from a practicing architect. If a source retracts or we get a fact wrong, we update the article and note the change — write to info@myvilla.la.
